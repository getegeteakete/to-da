理念Our Philosophy

時を、
受けとめる場所。A place that
holds time.

「急ぐことを、やめて。」“ Cease your hurry. ”

寛政の頃より、この山荘は旅人の身体と心をやわらかく解いてきました。二百三十年という歳月が刻んだ柱、磨かれた廊下、呼吸する土壁。私たちはそれらを、ただ保存するのではなく、今ふたたび「いのちが帰ってくる場所」として、静かに蘇らせます。 Since the late 18th century, this mountain retreat has quietly welcomed weary travelers. Two hundred and thirty years live inside these pillars, polished corridors, and breathing earthen walls. We do not merely preserve what stands — we invite it, gently, to become a home where the soul can return.

滞在The Stay

五感で、
森に還る。Return to
the Forest.

湯に、膳に、朝の気配に。山荘透雫の一日は、自然との静かな対話でつづられます。 In the bath, at the table, in the morning air — a day at Sanso Tōda is a quiet dialogue with nature.

Ⅰ  —  Onsen

湯 浴 みBathing Ritual

Yu-ami湯浴み — Yu-ami

山の伏流水でひかれた湯に、身を沈めれば、杉の香りがふわりと立ちのぼる。肌にやわらかな湯が、一日の疲れを、ほどいてゆきます。 Submerge yourself in waters drawn from the mountain's hidden springs. The scent of cedar rises gently, and the day's weight dissolves in water that falls soft upon the skin.

Ⅱ  —  Kaiseki

食 の 膳The Kaiseki Table

Zen — Kaiseki膳 — Zen

地元の漁師が夜明けに届ける玄界の魚。この山でしか採れない山菜と茸。季節が、一皿ごとにあらわれる、静かな会席料理。 Fish from the Genkai Sea, delivered by local fishermen at dawn. Wild vegetables and mushrooms found only on these slopes. The season quietly reveals itself, course by course.

Ⅲ  —  Retreat

寂 の 時 間The Hour of Silence

Jaku — Silence寂 — Jaku

朝の坐禅、森の瞑想歩行、静かに呼吸を整える時間。熟練の指導のもと、おのずと自分と向きあう、ウェルネスリトリート。 Morning meditation, forest walks that become prayer, hours set aside for breath. Guided retreats that bring one, without forcing, face to face with oneself.

建築Architecture

受け継がれた、
手の仕事。The Craft
Passed Down.

寛政七年に建てられた本棟を中心に、茶室、離れ、湯殿を、現代の職人の手で丁寧に蘇らせます。古き良きものを守りながら、今を生きる身体に寄りそう滞在のための設え。日本建築の美しさと、世界水準のもてなしが、ここで静かに出会います。 The main structure — built in 1795 — along with the tea rooms, detached pavilions, and bath houses, are being revived by artisans of our time. Preserving what is old and beautiful, while offering every comfort the modern body asks for. Here, traditional Japanese architecture meets hospitality of the highest world order.
